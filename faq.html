Setup & Production
Setup time depends on how much equipment is being used for the event. Smaller setups may require less time, while larger setups with lighting, trussing, sound, photo booth, special effects, or LED visuals may require more venue access. We will never arrive later than 1 hour and 30 minutes before the event start time so we can set up properly and do the job to the best of our ability.
The venue needs to provide reliable power and parking. Everything else depends on the event and setup, but power access and a reasonable place to park/load in are the main requirements we need to complete the job properly.
Yes. The client is responsible for giving us details about any venue restrictions. This includes rules about sound levels, fog, haze, cold sparks, lighting, load-in access, parking, timing, power, outdoor setup areas, or anything else that may affect the equipment or production.
Yes. We handle both indoor and outdoor events. For outdoor setups, the main requirement is a large enough and reliable power source for the equipment being used. Outdoor events may also require extra planning depending on weather, ground surface, distance from power, and equipment placement.
Before the event, we check the weather. If rain is expected, we will speak with the client and review the equipment that was rented. If any equipment is not waterproof or cannot be safely used in the weather conditions, we will not be able to provide that specific equipment and will issue a refund for the equipment that cannot be rented.
Yes. We can customize the build depending on the event type, venue, budget, guest count, available power, and desired look. For custom builds, contact us directly through the Contact page.
Rentals & Equipment
Yes. All rental equipment is delivered, set up, and picked up by Heat District Productions or an authorized team member. This helps make sure the equipment is installed correctly, safely placed, and ready for the event.
Only persons authorized by Heat District Productions are allowed to operate our equipment. If a DJ or production professional rents DJ equipment for a club, gig, or professional setup, they may be allowed to operate the approved equipment. However, guests or unauthorized persons are not allowed to operate production equipment such as LED walls, lighting systems, trussing setups, special effects, or other technical gear.
If equipment is damaged by a guest, misuse, unauthorized handling, or spilled drinks, the client may be responsible for repair, replacement, or the cash value of the damaged item. If the damage is caused by the venue or by weather conditions outside of the client’s control, the client is not responsible for reimbursing Heat District Productions.
Yes. DJ equipment rentals are available without booking a DJ. This may include items like CDJs, mixers, or all-in-one DJ systems depending on availability. Standalone rental pricing may differ from package add-on pricing.
Lighting and effects may only be rented for client-operated use when the renter has the proper experience or is running a professional setup. For most events, lighting, effects, trussing, LED walls, and similar production equipment must be operated or supervised by Heat District Productions or an approved operator to protect the equipment and keep the event safe.
